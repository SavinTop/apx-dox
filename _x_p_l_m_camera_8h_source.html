13/*
14 * XPLMCamera - THEORY OF OPERATION The XPLMCamera APIs allow plug-ins to
15 * control the camera angle in X-Plane. This has a number of applications,
16 * including but not limited to:
17 *
18 * - Creating new views (including dynamic/user-controllable views) for the
19 * user.
20 *
21 * - Creating applications that use X-Plane as a renderer of scenery,
22 * aircrafts, or both.
23 *
24 * The camera is controlled via six parameters: a location in OpenGL
25 * coordinates and pitch, roll and yaw, similar to an airplane's position.
26 * OpenGL coordinate info is described in detail in the XPLMGraphics
27 * documentation; generally you should use the XPLMGraphics routines to
28 * convert from world to local coordinates. The camera's orientation starts
29 * facing level with the ground directly up the negative-Z axis (approximately
30 * north) with the horizon horizontal. It is then rotated clockwise for yaw,
31 * pitched up for positive pitch, and rolled clockwise around the vector it is
32 * looking along for roll.
33 *
34 * You control the camera either either until the user selects a new view or
35 * permanently (the later being similar to how UDP camera control works). You
36 * control the camera by registering a callback per frame from which you
37 * calculate the new camera positions. This guarantees smooth camera motion.
38 *
39 * Use the XPLMDataAccess APIs to get information like the position of the
40 * aircraft, etc. for complex camera positioning.
41 *
42 */

80 * This structure contains a full specification of the camera. X, Y, and Z are

101 * You use an XPLMCameraControl function to provide continuous control over
